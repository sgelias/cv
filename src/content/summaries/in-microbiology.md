I have approximately twelve years of experience in the fields of Microbiology
and Mycology, working in major areas such as [Health Sciences](#health-sciences)
(where I started my career) and [Life Sciences](#life-sciences) (the area where
I have greater expertise and focus). My work in the field of Mycology is the
result of a trajectory built over eight years of continuous work in the area, in
partnership with leading professionals working in Brazil, such as Aristóteles
Góes-Neto [1], Elisandro Ricardo Drechsler-Santos [2], João Paulo Machado de
Araújo [3], José Carmine Dianese [4], Maria Alice Neves [5] and Robert W.
Barreto [6] (all mycologists of national and international renown). Over this
time, I have also established research partnerships with international
scientists, such as Charles W. Barnes [7], Mary Catherine Aime [8], Harry C.
Evans [9] and Dominik Begerow [10]. I consider all of them my direct and
indirect mentors in the field of Mycology. My areas of interest are genetics,
taxonomy, ecology and evolution of microorganisms, focusing mainly on issues
related to wild fungi and bacteria (organisms without commercial interest),
pathogens of agricultural and environmental interest.

I have deep knowledge in analysis using biostatistical and bioinformatic
techniques (see section [Bioinformatics](#bioinformatics)), with a focus on
analyses of genomic and metagenomic data.

I am passionate and driven by scientific challenges and the discovery of the
mysteries of the natural world. Visit the [Publications](#publications) section
to see details of the scientific trajectory I have followed.
